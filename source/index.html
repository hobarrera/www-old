About me

I'm an IT consultant and soft computing researcher. I'm very passionate about programming, unix, security, and somewhat obsessed with standards compliance. I do web, desktop and mobile software design and development, in a few different languages.

I'm currently working as a software developer for Assia Inc., as a researcher at the University of Palermo and I also do consulting on my own, formerly under the brand of ubertech.

I use ArchLinux on the desktop (and laptops) and OpenBSD (mostly on servers). My browser of choice is Firefox (in great part due to their openness, but also due to issues that I can't stand in other browsers. I also love their dev tools), I use Sublime Text for small/medium projects, and Eclipse for Java stuff (and most work-related stuff). I use vi a lot for quickly editing files. I take notes in class with Xournal, because I can switch between typing and drawing equations/graphs (using a Bamboo Connect) quickly and keep things tidy. I've never gained enough familiarity or speed with Latex to use it in class, though I wish I had the time!

I sometimes get weird looks since I use Mutt for email, but, after finding it to be faster and easier than any web-based or X-based email client around, I can't see myself moving to anything else. It also has the added advantage that I always have a full copy of all my email locally at all times. I do have an issue with managing contacts though, since I can't find anything that's not broken in some critical aspect. I use Pidgin (with XMPP) exclusively for chat, and XMPP is my mail protocol.

On a more personal note, I love cooking, and I do so a great deal. Due to my love for openness, and strong conviction that knowledge should always be shared, it's only natural that I've started publishing some recipes for stuff I do. I don't really have much spare time though, so that section is pretty poor. I'm also a sentiocentrist and experimenting with polyphasic sleep.
