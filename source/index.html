About me

I'm an IT consultant and soft computing researcher. I'm very passionate about programming, unix, security, and somewhat obsessed with standards compliance. I do web, desktop and mobile software design and development, in a few different languages.

I'm currently working as a software developer for Assia Inc., as a researcher at the University of Palermo and I also do consulting on my own under the brand of ubertech.

I use ArchLinux on the desktop (and most laptops) and OpenBSD (mostly on servers). My browser of choice is Firefox (mostly chosen because it's the one that has the least thing that annoy me), I use Sublime Text for small/medium projects, and Eclipse for Java stuff (like most work-related stuff). I use vi a lot for quickly editing files. I use Xournal to take notes in class, because I can switch between typing and drawing equations/graphs (using a Bamboo Connect) quickly and keep things tidy. I've never gained enough familiarity or speed with Latex to use it in class.

I sometimes get weird looks since I use Mutt for email, but I've found it to be faster and easier than any web-based or X-based email client around. It also has the added advantage that I always have a full copy of all my email locally. I do have an issue with managing contacts though, since I can't find anything that's not broken in some way. I use Pidgin (with XMPP) exclusively for chat.
